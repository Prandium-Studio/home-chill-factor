About the score

Tonight's score is the sum of several factors, capped at 10. Higher = more reason to light the fire.

WAT  0 – 8.0 pts

Weighted average temperature across 9pm – 7am, biased toward the coldest pre-dawn hours (3–6am carry the most weight). The headline "how cold is the night" reading.

Wind  0 – 2.0 pts

Worst single-hour gap between actual and "feels like" temp overnight, multiplied by a direction factor. SSE–WNW winds (150°–315°) carry full weight; N–NE winds carry half.

Cloud  0 – 0.8 pts

Average cloud cover 1pm – 5pm. Heavy cloud = little solar warmth absorbed by the building during the day = a colder starting point for the night.

Drop  0 – 1.5 pts

Temperature fall from 4pm to 9pm. A steep drop means the building is bleeding heat fast as evening sets in.

Soak  0 – 1.5 pts

Average wind chill gap across 9am – 4pm. Persistent daytime wind that has been stripping heat from the building before nightfall. Only shown when active.

Eve  0 – 1.0 pts

Average wind chill gap across 4pm – 9pm. Wind still actively cooling the building as you decide whether to light. Only shown when active.

Dampness  0 – 0.5 pts

Combined effect of high overnight humidity and heavy afternoon cloud — adds a small penalty when the house feels damp on top of cold.
